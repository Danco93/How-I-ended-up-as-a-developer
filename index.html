I'm Daniel Council, a Full Stack Web Developer.

This is my story, but if you'd like to skip ahead to my work history, feel free to click this link.

Early Days

I went to college in 2011 fulltime while also working fulltime to be able to afford school. At the time I was pursuing a bachelors in bioimedical engineering. I eventually switched to math education, and then one final time dropping the education concentration and focusing more on theotretical mathematics and finishing with a B.A. in mathematics.

My plan at the time was to finish school and try and land a role as a data analyst or jr. data scientist, but as they say "the best liad plans of mice and men often go awry." I graduated in 2016 and was unable to find a job in data analytics but what I did find was a role in managment for Amazon.com"

E-Commerce and Operations

Over the next 5 years I worked my way up from a manager in operations at amazon to other manager and operations based roles for RGBSI and Chewy.com. I learned a lot about managing people and processes, but I was still not satisfied.

I wanted to be able to use my degree and work with data. I started looking for jobs in data analytics and data science, but I was still not able to find anything. My thoughts then started to become more along the lines of pursuing a small business in a field I had always been pasionate about - media. The way I saw it was, it was better to fail now taking a chance than later on in life when I had kids and maybe this could fill me with the sense of purpose I had been looking for.

Small-Business and Self Discovery

So, I started a small media company and began to learn about photography, videography, and editing. I started to learn about marketing and how to build a brand. I started to learn about entrepreneurship and how to build a business. I started to learn about myself and what I wanted to do with my life. I had a lot of knowledge on photography already but learned a ton about videography and met so many cool people.

I toured across the country with all sorts of bands and did a quite a few weddings - you can see some of my pictures in my hobbies page, and I'm thankful for that time but unfortunatley it just wasn't financially lucrative enough to do it full time after a couple years. Ultimately, I had to make a decision to either go back to working in operations or find a new career path. I was still not satisfied of the idea of going back to working in operations and I was still not able to find a job in data analytics or data science. I started to look into other options and started to think about coding, web development, and software engineering. I started to learn about coding and web development and started to learn about software engineering. Around this time I met a photographer - who was also a devleoper - in the industry who told me he thought I really could land a role in the field if I really commited and taught myself. So, that night, I made the commitment.

Failure and The Climb

After I promised myself I would learn to code, I started to learn about coding and web development. I started to learn about software engineering. I took a job as a team lead with a medical manufacturing company to make enough money to pay my bills but leave me enough free time after work to study coding and web development. I started to learn about html, css, and javascript. I started to learn about react, node.js, and express. I started to learn about databases and how to build a full stack application. I started to learn about how to build a website and how to build a web application. I learned about the freedom a career in software development could give me and how I could work from anywhere in the world. I started to learn about myself and what I wanted to do with my life.

Doing all of this at a fairly slow pace for the first year as a team lead around 11 months in, I decided to make a daily commitment to code at least 2 hours per day every day and be a web developer by 2027. The team lead role was great, but I knew from day one, it felt like a way to exist for me, and not a way to live. Coding, web development, and software engineering was a way to live. Seeing the engagment it gave to me mentally was so encouraging, and the possibiltiy of implenting it for my hobbies and other passions, culminated in this epiphany of "I wish I would have tried this 10 years ago."

The Path Ahead

I'm still a team lead, for a medical manufacturing company, but I am also a self-taught web developer and activley seeking employment in the field. I am currently working on a few projects to build my portfolio and gain experience. You can see those here, but am hoping to land a role in the field by 2027. I am also looking to work on some open-source projects and contribute to the community. I am also looking to learn more about coding and web development and software engineering.

feel like it took me a long time to find my way, but I am finally on the right path. I am excited about the future and what it holds. If you've made it this far in reading about my story, I hope you see someone who's got a lot of experience across many fields and is not just a "coder" but a leader, an self-starter and someone who is passionate about what they do. I hope you see someone who is not just looking for a job, but looking for a career and a way to make a difference in the world. I hope you see someone who is not just looking for a paycheck, but looking for a way to make a living doing what they love. I hope you see someone who is not just looking for a job, but looking for a way to make a difference in the world through coding.
